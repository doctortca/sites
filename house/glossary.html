Adjustable Rate Mortgage (ARM)
A mortgage with an intersest rate that fluctuates depending on current interest rates and a preselected index.

Amorization
At the beginning of a loan, most of your monthly payment goes toward paying off interest. As the loan's age increases, more an more of your monthly payment goes toward your principle.

Annual Percentage Rate (APR)
The annual interest rate for a loan, including points and other fees.

Appraisal
An estimate by a real estate appraiser of the market value of your home.

Closing Costs
Costs paid on closing day other than down payment. This can include fees for loan origination, credit reports, escrow, hazard insurance, and real estate appraisal.

Debt-to-Income Ratio
The ratio of the borrower's debts to his or her income. The acceptable ratio varies depending on your lender. But generally, monthly mortgage payments should not exceed 30% of your net income, and your total debt (home loan included) should not exceed 38%.

Down Payment
The amount of money a borrower puts down on a home at closing. This is generally anywhere from 5% to 20%.

Earnest Money
Money paid in advance to seller or builder to indicate you are serious about purchasing their home. This amount sometimes is non-refundable.

Escrow
An account held by the lender that contains money paid in advance by the borrower for insurance, tax, and sometimes earnest money.

Fixed Rate Mortage
Mortgages in which the interest rate is fixed for the life of the loan.

Hazard Insurance
Insurance to protect home against fire, violent storms, and other catastrophes.

Index
Rate for which changes to adjustable rate mortgage interest rates are based.

Interest Rate
The percentage of the total loan value paid by the borrower.

Loan Application Fee
Fee required by lenders for loan application processing. This can be as high as $300 depending on the lender.

Lock-In
A set interest rate guaranteed by the lender for a specified period of time before closing occurs.

Market Rate
The current average interest rate charged by lenders for fixed-rate mortgages.

Points
One point is equal to 1% of the total loan amount. A certain number of points are sometimes charged to home loan borrowers by lenders at closing.

Principal
The total amount of a home loan, not including interest.

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)
Insurance to protect the lender against default on a loan. This is required for all loans in which the borrower pays less than 20% down.

Title
A document indicating property ownership.

Title Company
The company that will insure the title on your home.

Underwriting
The process by which a lender determines whether a potential borrower will be a good credit risk. The borrower's finanical history is evaluated, and the property to be purchased is appraised for fair market value.
