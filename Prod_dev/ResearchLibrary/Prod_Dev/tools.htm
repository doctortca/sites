All of the tools above can be used to deliver our courses. The main differences between them are:

  • Type of media supported (video, audio, animation, text, graphics)
  • Ease of use (level of technical ability required of the author)
  • Development and maintenance speed (regardless of author’s technical aptitude)
  • Delivery platform and compatibility with other programs, file formats, and systems

About digital media:

  • Studies show that people prefer viewing clear yet choppy streaming video to fuzzy, smooth video.

About training technology:

  •  "Avoid using the same tool for every program... Each [tool] can offer wonderful training possibilities, but no single tool will be equally effective for solving all training problems. Use a combination." -- Margaret Driscoll

  • Avoid selecting your tool before defining the training need. The problem here is that you must work in "reverse" and locate course content that will "fit" into the tool. Define training gaps and conduct an analysis of training needs before purchasing tools.

"My strongest advice is to design first and then buy the tool that best realizes that design. If you start with a tool, you are limited to what it can do. The history of design is full of unsuccessful examples of designing to fit that box." -- William Horton

  • Beware of tools that claim they are so advanced that they can "design" content for you. Putting ill-designed content up on the Web is still ill-designed content no matter what tool you use.

  • Selecting the right tool for development should depend on the technical ability of your developers. 

    There's no reason to use extremely simplistic tools (that claim time-savings but may cost you a fortune), only to wind up with a tool that creates cookie-cutter courses, doesn't offer the functionality & flexibility more advanced tools may offer, or requires a lengthy learning curve in order for developers to become comfortable using it.

RealMedia - This is a Web streaming format that's been around since 1997. It includes RealAudio and RealVideo (used together, it’s called RealMedia). Real has the some of the best tools to get audio/video online quickly. 

PWPL is a RealNetwork Partner, which allows us to hear about the latest and greatest product releases and a receive more advanced technical support than the average customer. We can also make recommendations about products and services.

advantages: 

  • Has SureStream technology - Allows us to encode the audio/video once and create a file that can accommodate multiple bit rates/connections. Bit rates include 28.8 & greater modems, T1s, xDSLs, and cable modems. The RealPlayer and RealServer intelligently determine the connection rate and give you the best possible audio/video feed. As network congestion occurs, the player and server continue that stream at the appropriate bit rate. The end user gets an enjoyable viewing experience without having to deal with technical issues.
  • Integrates well with SMIL technology
  • Runs OK on Windows and Macintosh platforms

disadvantages:

  • Player is somewhat buggy. There is hope that this will be resolved when the full version of Real Player 8 comes out (Beware of Real Player 8 beta, however! Currently it has considerable "bugs".)

QuickTime - QuickTime is the oldest digital video architecture around, and serves as the foundation for the entire digital video industry. It’s been doing digital video on the Web (downloadable) for many years, and just recently has started supporting streaming.

advantages: 

  • Very reliable for most uses because it’s been around a long time
  • Quality is generally very good
  • Historically has best support for rich media
  • Great for CD-ROMs and archived Web content
  • Offers progressive downloads
  •  Integrates well with SMIL
  • Runs well in Windows and Mac platforms

disadvantages:

  • While great for CD-ROMs and archived Web content, live streaming of media has been somewhat buggy
  • Does not have “SureStream”-type of alternative, we have to create different versions at different bit rates

Windows Media - The newest of the 3 video streaming technologies, and is a much simpler solution than either Real or QuickTime because it considers itself merely a “component” of a Web browser.

advantages:

  • Has a good quality codec
  • Runs well under Windows platforms
  • Offers progressive downloads

disadvantages:

  • Doesn’t offer additional multimedia support (like Real and QuickTime do); assumes the video is just a “piece” of a Web page. (Synchronous control over elements other than the video must be accomplished through DHTML or adding tags to ASF files).
  • Doesn’t really have a true “SureStream” alternative

MPEG-1 - WebOpedia gives a great definition of MPEG-1. PWPL uses MPEG-1 video for its CD-ROMs and some online distribution. At a high bit rate, MPEG-1 produces very good images. 

"Thinned" MPEG allows you to stream MPEG-1 video over the Internet. PWPL is considering using Thinned MPEG in its Thunderbolt product. 

tech notes: 

  • You can only get about 1 hour of MPEG-1 video on a single CD-ROM.
  • PWPL uses the Moviemaker100 MPEG capture card to encode MPEG-1 video. 

Generation21 - A Web-based course authoring tool that provides instructional design support as well as pre-built templates and course structure. 

advantages:

  • Contains pre-made templates for screen layout, course structure, and interactions (little or no technical aptitude required for use)
  • Uses fairly open architecture: output is HTML and JavaScript
  • Gen21 helps you to output instructionally sound courses because of its rigid structure
  • Fits with PWPL’s existing instructional systems design model for video-based and satellite courses (ISD or "ADDIE": Analyze, Design, Develop, Implement, Evaluate)

disadvantages:

  • While Gen21 values good instruction highly, it also requires a course to be broken down instructionally into very small components. The components are so small that it will take us considerable time to redesign courses for delivery in this format.
  • Gen21 does not allow flexibility in course design; format is set
Macromedia Dreamweaver w/ CourseBuilder - Dreamweaver is a Web page design & development tool (or HTML editor). When combined with its CourseBuilder extension, Dreamweaver allows you to create Web-based learning and interactivity.

advantages:

  • Allows great flexibility in course instructional design as well as layout
  • Uses an “open” architecture; output is pure HTML and JavaScript. This is good for two reasons:
    • Future authors won’t need CourseBuilder to edit the course.
    • Courses will be compatible with any system that accepts standard Web pages
  • Authors can create Web interactivity without needing to know JavaScript or HTML

disadvantages:

  • This tool requires some technical aptitude about Web development in general
  • Is so flexible that it may be easy for developers to make mistakes in the code
  • Without instructional design being performed up front, a course could be produced that isn’t instructionally sound
  • Because of the above, quality assurance and testing cannot be taken lightly
Macromedia Flash - A Web-based multimedia and interactivity tool that allows custom creation of Web content.

advantages: 

  • Structure is so flexible, you can develop anything you dream up
  • Can develop full-multimedia, full-interactivity applications
  • Small file size, short time to download

disadvantages:

  • Requires some technical aptitude about Web development in general
  • Requires graphic design ability
  • Development takes time

SMIL - Web programming language developed by the World Wide Web Consortium, or W3C (the primary standard-setting body for the Internet), that allows authors to create interactive multimedia presentations. SMIL stands for “Synchronized Multimedia Integrated Language” and is a subset of XML (Extensible Markup Language).

advantages:

  • Allows full control of synchronization of multimedia content over the Web
  • Is supported by Real Player and QuickTime

disadvantages:

  • Technical aptitude and time is needed currently to incorporate SMIL into Web pages. However, new tools are being developed that will hopefully make the process easier and faster in the future.

SightPath - SightPath is a satellite technology that allows us to bypass the bandwidth constraints of the Internet and deliver high-quality, rich media to the desktop. We are currently beta testing SightPath with several of our customers in various industries, including Healthcare and Law Enforcement.

Netpodium - An application that allows live Webcasts and interactive polling and questioning.

advantages:

  • Good for live broadcasts

disadvantages:

  • Archived content capability

DVD - Digital Video Disc or Digital Versatile Disc is the next generation of optical disc storage technology. It's a bigger, faster “CD” that can hold video as well as audio and computer data.  

advantages:

  • Video quality is flawless
  • Offers simple interactivity
  • Can be viewed on a computer or TV
  • Recent developments allow Web delivery

disadvantages:

  • Not capable of complex learning interactions
