Studies show that students have a much higher knowledge-retention rate when content is presented in a variety of media. This is not only due to research on individual learning styles (our tendency to “prefer” or respond better to one presentation method over another), but also to the fact that students actually learn more when presented with information that stimulates more than one of their senses. The reason is that as our brains process incoming information, cognitive pathways are created in the brain that allow us later to retrieve that information. The more pathways created for a specific piece of information, the easier it will be for us to recall that information later.

This puts PRIMEDIA in a unique position. We already have thousands of video-based titles that do just that. They promote high retention rates and motivation by stimulating multiple senses through great visuals and audio.

But we can take that one step further. With the advent of the Internet, we have a whole new world of opportunity open to us.

One of the best methods for stimulating a variety of senses is WBT, which can stimulate not only visual and auditory processing in the brain through use of video, audio, animation, graphics, and text, but also can help students practice and apply what they’ve learned through frequent interactivity.
