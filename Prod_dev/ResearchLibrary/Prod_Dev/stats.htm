• Students prefer the following (in this order) over all other teaching methods in WBT:

    • examples 

    • content

    • assignments

    • instruction

    • Also, further down the list: PDFs - Students like the ability to have a “print-friendly” document so they can have the flexibility to study it away from the computer

  • We learn 1% of what we know through taste, and:

1.5% through touch

3.5% through smell

11% through hearing

83% through sight

  • We *remember* 10% of what we read, and:

20% of what we hear

30% of what we see

50% of what we see and hear

80% of what we say

90% of what we say as we act

  • Chinese Proverb: I hear and I forget, I see and I remember, I do and I understand, and one picture is worth a thousand words.

     

  • Stats about teaching or instructing others:

    • Telling, when used alone, results in 70% recall 3 hours later, and 10% recall 3 days later

    • Showing, when used alone, results in 72% recall 3 hours later, and 20% recall 3 days later

    • Blend of telling and showing results in 85% 3 hours later and 65% recall 3 days later

  • 90% of what people are calling “instruction” is really just information.

  • CD-ROM-based training actually increased last year.

  • It used to be that customers wanted products “Cheaper, Better, and Faster”.

    But now, because things are moving at "Internet speed", customers want products “Free, Perfect, and Now”:

    • We used to have 4-6 months to develop a course.

    • Development cycles are shortening every year by 20%.

    • It is projected that by 2004, we will only get 2-3 days to develop a course!
  • The primary reasons people quit WBTs:

    • Poor personal time management skills 

    • Organizations usually don’t allot time for WBT-type training like they do for classroom training

    • Feelings of anonymity
